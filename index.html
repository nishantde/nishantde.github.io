Design Tools

As a product designer, I have mainly been using Figma to create wireframes and prototypes. Figma's intuitive user interface makes it easy to create shapes and objects, and its cloud platform allows me to access my projects and files from the desktop client as well as the browser. Its vast community offers many extensions that can be seamlessly duplicated and added to files.

Additionally, I also love using Adobe XD. Like Figma, I would classify its UI as being accessible, and it offers a wide support for external plugins that integrate very well with just about every project. Balsamiq, while easy to use, is not easy on the eyes; I found that designing wireframes was quicker due to its focus on mostly low-fidelity designs and existing device frames, but it made it harder for most users to get accustomed to its visual aesthetics. This often reminds me of one of the most important tenets of Human-Computer Interaction: users don't make good designers and designers don't make good users!
